01
Brand Story

The journey to net zero starts with a single route.

Route to Zero is a movement, a commitment, and a visual shorthand for the decarbonisation path the transport and energy sectors must travel. The identity captures momentum — a downward trajectory toward zero emissions, enclosed in the confidence of a road sign.

Brand Essence

The logo is rooted in two metaphors: the familiar shape of a road sign — communicating direction, purpose, and a clear destination — and a declining graph arrow traversing a globe, representing measurable progress toward a global goal.

Design Principles

Bold — High contrast, strong geometry, unapologetic weight.
Directional — Every element points toward zero.
Grounded — Industrial confidence, not aspirational softness.
Global — The sphere signals worldwide ambition.

08
Logo Misuse

What not to do.

Protect the mark. Respect the system. These examples are prohibited in all contexts.

Do not place on gradients that reduce contrast.
Do not distort or stretch. Scale proportionally.
Do not rotate. Always remain horizontal.
Do not reduce opacity or apply transparency.
Do not alter brand colours or apply filters.
Do not crop or cut off any element.
09
Imagery & Photography

A documentary lens on the transition.

Photography should feel authentic, industrial and optimistic — capturing the people, places and infrastructure driving decarbonisation. Avoid stock cliché. Favour natural light, wide horizons, and the textures of real work.

01 — HeroRenewable energy at scale
02 — InfrastructureSolar & clean generation
03 — MobilityElectric transport
04 — EnvironmentNature & offset
05 — RouteAerial & landscape
06 — PeopleWorkers & operators

Rules of thumb

Do

Use natural light, documentary framing, and real locations. Favour wide depth-of-field and muted, cool colour grading. Show scale, movement, and human presence.

Don't

Avoid staged stock photography, heavy saturation, lens flares, posed handshakes, and clipart-style illustrations. No AI-generated faces without disclosure.

Treatment

Apply a subtle teal-to-ink gradient overlay on hero images when paired with text. Keep contrast high enough for the lime accent and white type to remain legible.
